Two rules: single-word frequency adverbs sit in a fixed mid-sentence slot; multiple adverbial phrases at the end follow manner → place → frequency → time.

    黏土風文法解說片：wish Wish Clauses, No If-Clause at All

    假設語氣的第二個親戚，也是整個系列的完結篇。wish + 過去式講現在的願望，wish + had + 過去分詞講過去的懊悔，wish + would 則通常不是講自己，是抱怨別人一直重複做某件事——這是最容易被忽略的第三種用法。 The second "relative," and the finale of the whole series. Wish + past tense for a present wish, wish + had + past participle for past regret, and wish + would — usually not about yourself, but a complaint about someone's repeated behavior, the most overlooked of the three uses.
