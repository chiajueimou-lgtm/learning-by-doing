中文母語者常見的六個英文文法陷阱 Six Common Grammar Traps for Chinese-Native English Speakers

這篇不是從我某一句寫錯的話開始的，是反過來問：以我目前展現出來的程度、加上「中文母語者」這個變因，統計上最可能還沒發現的雷區會是什麼？六個都整理進來了。 This one didn't start from a mistake I actually made — it's the reverse: given my demonstrated level and the fact that Chinese is my native language, what are the statistically most likely blind spots I haven't caught yet? All six are here.

這六個陷阱背後其實只有兩個病根：①中文的動詞不隨主詞變化、名詞不分單複數，導致「主詞動詞一致」「可數/不可數」這類規則對中文母語者來說幾乎是隱形的；②中文句子習慣「意合」——用逗號把幾個念頭串在一起、或用「主題，評論」的結構——這兩種習慣直接搬進英文都會出問題。 These six traps trace back to just two root causes: Chinese verbs don't change with the subject and nouns don't mark singular/plural, so rules like subject-verb agreement and countability are nearly invisible to Chinese-native intuition. And Chinese sentences favor "parataxis" — stringing ideas together with commas, or a topic-then-comment structure — both of which cause trouble when carried directly into English.

陷阱一 · COMMA SPLICE

逗號拼接句 Comma Splices

兩個各自完整的句子，不能只用一個逗號接在一起

跟你已經問過的「and 前逗號規則」是同一個家族。中文習慣把幾個念頭用逗號串起來（意合），直接搬進英文文法上是錯的——兩個獨立子句中間，逗號後面要嘛加連接詞（and、but、so⋯），要嘛換成分號，要嘛乾脆拆成兩句。 Same family as the "and" comma rule you already covered. Chinese habitually strings ideas together with commas; carried directly into English, that's grammatically wrong — two independent clauses need a conjunction after the comma, a semicolon, or a full stop between them.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    I finished the report, I sent it to the team.
    兩個都是完整句子（各自有主詞動詞），中間只用逗號接，是逗號拼接句。
  • ✓ 正確 — 加連接詞
    I finished the report, and I sent it to the team.
  • ✓ 正確 — 換分號
    I finished the report; I sent it to the team.
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜）— 找出邏輯關係，換連接詞
    The meeting ran long, so we didn't have time to discuss the budget.
    原本兩句只用逗號接會是拼接句；加上 so，順便把「因為⋯所以⋯」的邏輯講清楚，比單純加 and 更好。
陷阱二 · SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT

主詞動詞一致 Subject-Verb Agreement

動詞要跟「真正的主詞」一致，不是跟離它最近的名詞一致

中文動詞完全不隨主詞變化，這整條規則對中文母語者來說幾乎是「看不見」的。最容易出錯的情況：主詞和動詞中間隔了一長串修飾語，一不小心就跟旁邊的名詞而不是真正的主詞一致。 Chinese verbs never change with the subject, so this entire rule category is nearly invisible to Chinese-native intuition. The most common trap: a long modifying phrase sits between the subject and the verb, and the verb ends up agreeing with the wrong, nearby noun.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    The list of items are on the desk.
    動詞被拉去跟離它最近的 items（複數）一致了，但真正的主詞是 list（單數）。
  • ✓ 正確
    The list of items is on the desk.
  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    One of the reports were missing.
    主詞是 one（單數），不是 reports。
  • ✓ 正確
    One of the reports was missing.
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜）— 集合名詞
    The team is working on the proposal this week.
    team、company、committee 這類集合名詞，美式英文通常當單數看待（英式偶爾允許複數，但商業書信照美式規則最保險）。
陷阱三 · COUNTABLE vs UNCOUNTABLE

可數 vs 不可數名詞 Countable vs. Uncountable Nouns

不可數名詞永遠不能加 s，也不能直接用 a/an

跟冠詞是同一個病根：中文名詞不分單複數，這幾個字幾乎每個中文母語者都加過 s。商業書信最常中招的幾個字：information、advice、feedback、equipment、furniture、news、progress。 Same root cause as articles: Chinese nouns don't mark singular/plural, so these specific words get pluralized almost universally by Chinese-native speakers. The worst offenders in business email: information, advice, feedback, equipment, furniture, news, progress.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    Please send me some informations.
  • ✓ 正確
    Please send me some information.
  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    I have three advices for you.
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜）
    Let me give you a piece of advice, or better, some advice about the proposal.
    真的需要講「幾個」不可數名詞的量，要用 a piece of / pieces of 這種量詞包裝，不能直接加 s。
商業書信最常見的不可數名詞正確用法
information, advice, feedback, equipment, furniture, news, progress, software不加 s，量詞用 some / a piece of / a lot of
陷阱四 · UNTIL vs BY

deadline 用 until 還是 by "Until" vs. "By" for Deadlines

by + 時間 = 截止期限（一次性動作要在那之前完成）；until + 時間 = 持續到那個時間點為止

你每天在寫給全公司的截止日期信，這個如果用錯，影響可能比冠詞還直接。差別在動作的性質：「交報告」是一次性完成的動作，用 by；「辦公室關閉」是持續的狀態，用 until。 You write deadline emails to the whole company daily, so this one has direct real-world impact. The distinction: "by" for a one-time action that must be complete at or before that point; "until" for a state or action that continues up to that point.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    Please submit the report until Friday.
    字面意思變成「持續交報告，交到星期五為止」——但交報告是一次性動作，這樣講不合理。
  • ✓ 正確
    Please submit the report by Friday.
  • ✓ 正確 — until 用對的地方
    The office will be closed until Friday.
    「關閉」是持續的狀態，一路持續到星期五，這裡 until 才是對的。
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜，兩者對比放在同一句）
    The system will be under maintenance until 6 PM, so please submit all requests by 5 PM.
    系統維護是持續狀態到六點（until），但送出請求是一次性動作、要提前完成（by）。
陷阱五 · EMBEDDED QUESTIONS

間接問句語序 Word Order in Indirect (Embedded) Questions

問句包進句子裡當子句時，語序要還原成「主詞 + 動詞」，不能保留問句的倒裝

中文問句跟直述句語序完全一樣（「他在哪裡」跟「我不知道他在哪裡」語序不變），但英文問句會倒裝（Where is he?），包進另一個句子當子句時，卻要「還原」回直述句語序——這個反直覺的地方，很多中文母語者一輩子沒發現。 Chinese question word order is identical whether standalone or embedded. English inverts for standalone questions (Where is he?) but must revert to statement order once embedded in another sentence — a counter-intuitive switch many Chinese speakers never catch.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    I don't know where is he.
    保留了問句的倒裝語序（is he），但這裡是包在 "I don't know" 裡的子句，要還原成直述句語序。
  • ✓ 正確
    I don't know where he is.
  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    Could you tell me what time does the meeting start?
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜）
    Could you tell me what time the meeting starts, and whether it has been confirmed?
    連著兩個間接問句都要用直述句語序：what time the meeting starts（不是 does the meeting start），whether it has been confirmed（不是問句倒裝）。
陷阱六 · TOPIC-COMMENT TRANSFER

主題—評論結構殘留 Topic-Comment Structure Carried Over from Chinese

英文一個子句只能有一個主詞——不能先講一個「主題」，後面子句又重複一個代名詞當主詞

中文很自然會先丟出一個主題，再用一個完整句子去評論它（「我爸爸，他在銀行工作」）。這個結構直接搬進英文，會變成同一個子句裡有兩個主詞（father 跟 he），文法上是錯的——英文要嘛把代名詞拿掉，要嘛整句重新組織。 Chinese naturally states a topic, then comments on it with a full clause that repeats a pronoun subject. Carried directly into English, this creates a double subject in one clause — ungrammatical. English either drops the redundant pronoun or restructures the sentence entirely.

  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    My father, he works in a bank.
    father 跟 he 其實是同一個人，卻在同一個子句裡出現兩次當主詞。
  • ✓ 正確
    My father works in a bank.
    直接把多餘的代名詞拿掉就對了。
  • ✗ 錯誤示範
    The report that I sent yesterday, it has some errors.
  • ✓ 正確（較複雜，需要整句重組）
    The new policy, everyone thinks it is unfair.
    → Everyone thinks the new policy is unfair. 這種情況不能只是刪代名詞，要把整句重新組織，讓 the new policy 變成從屬子句裡的受詞，而不是句首單獨晾著的主題。
